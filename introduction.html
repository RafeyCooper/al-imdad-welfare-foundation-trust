About - Introduction
Introduction
Al Imdad Welfare Foundation Trust is a non-profit entity committed to advancing social welfare, education, and humanitarian assistance. Founded on January 1, 2011, originally under the name Marakiz Taleemul Qur'an Trust, the organization underwent a rebranding for formal recognition, thereby expanding its charitable endeavors. The Trust is devoted to enhancing communities by offering high-quality education, clean water, job opportunities, healthcare, and social services to marginalized populations across Pakistan. Focusing strongly on Islamic and modern education, Al Imdad Welfare Foundation Trust runs multiple schools, madrassas, and vocational training centers in various locales, ensuring that children receive comprehensive education that equips them for both spiritual and occupational success. Furthermore, the Trust has played a crucial role in disaster relief efforts, delivering assistance to flood-affected individuals, implementing water supply initiatives, and promoting social welfare programs such as mass marriages and job creation efforts. Through its diverse projects in education, healthcare, employment generation, and infrastructure advancement, Al Imdad Welfare Foundation Trust remains dedicated to fostering a more thriving and self-reliant society. Its mission is firmly rooted in the values of compassion, service, and empowerment, ensuring that those in need receive the essential support to lead dignified and fulfilling lives.
More
Al Imdad Welfare Foundation Trust is a non-profit organization dedicated to delivering a range of social and educational services. The Trust plays a pivotal role in enhancing society through various philanthropic endeavors, educational initiatives, and charitable activities. Established on January 1, 2011, as the 'Marakiz Taleemul Qur'an Trust', it was initiated with the intent of promoting education and social welfare. The organization was later rebranded as 'Al Imdad Welfare Foundation Trust' for legal purposes, which facilitated an expansion of its charitable efforts.
1. Educational Initiatives
The primary mission of Al Imdad Welfare Foundation Trust is to develop an educational framework that combines religious and contemporary education for children, enabling them to become proficient and valuable members of society.
Islamic and Contemporary Education System
The Trust has established numerous Islamic schools and madrassas across various regions, where children receive Quranic education alongside modern academic learning. The objective is to provide them with both religious and secular knowledge to help them better understand and meet their social and spiritual obligations.

- First School in Auwsta Muhammad (Baluchistan): This was the inaugural educational project of the Trust, aimed at delivering free and high-quality education to children.
- Schools in Karachi and Sindh: Additional schools have been instituted in Hazara Goth, Gulshan Iqbal Block 11, Bhanguria Goth, and Azizabad within Karachi.
- Tando Alahyar: Schools have also been established in other areas of interior Sindh, facilitating educational access for children in underprivileged regions.
Madrasas and Schools
- Establishment of Qur'anic Schools in Deer: 26 Qur'anic schools have been inaugurated in the Deer region, providing separate educational settings for boys and girls.
- Construction of Madrasa at Ahmedpor Sharqia: The Trust procured land and successfully built a Madrasa in this area, which is currently operational.
- Madrasa on the route to Kaghan: A Madrasa for girls has been established in the Jarir Shariah area, where they receive religious education.
2. Social Welfare Projects
Support for Flood Victims
The Trust has undertaken extensive efforts to assist those affected by floods. Relief supplies, including food, clothing, and medicine, were distributed to victims of the 2012 floods in Maripur, Bhag Nari, Aosta Muhammad, and various other regions in Sindh.
- PKR 50 to 60 lakh in relief materials distributed: The Trust provided extensive relief supplies valued in the lakhs to meet the immediate needs of those affected by the floods.
- Medical Camps: Medical camps were organized for flood victims, with doctors traveling to remote areas to offer free treatment and medications.
Clean Water Initiatives
The Trust addresses the critical issue of clean water supply in multiple regions.
- Installation of Over 150 Pumps and Wells: More than 150 water pumps and wells have been established across Sindh, Thar, Baluchistan, Karachi, Lakki Marwat, and Buner, ensuring access to clean drinking water for underserved areas.
- Water Tanks and Solar Systems: The Trust has fulfilled the need for water tanks in Thar, with plans for the installation of solar systems to enhance water supply.
Facilitating Marriages
The Trust has organized marriages for individuals who face financial barriers to getting married.
- Coordination of 20 Weddings: The Trust facilitated 20 marriages that were stalled due to financial and social challenges, providing support in reconnecting families and assisting with wedding expenses.
- Streamlined Marriage Process: This initiative aims to simplify marriage procedures and alleviate social pressures, enabling individuals to fulfill their social and religious obligations without undue difficulties.
3. Employment Provision
The Al Imdad Welfare Foundation Trust has launched various initiatives to create employment opportunities and foster self-sufficiency.
- Sewing Machines and Training: Women have been provided with sewing machines and training, empowering them with self-employment capabilities.
- Computer Institute Establishment: An institute for computer education has been created to equip youth with modern technology skills, enhancing their employment prospects.
- Job Creation for Hundreds: The Trust has generated employment for numerous individuals in various villages, with salaries funded directly by the organization.
4. Construction Projects
The Al Imdad Welfare Foundation Trust is also involved in the construction of mosques and madrasas.
- Completion of Jamia Tayyaba Islamia Madrasa: A new floor has been completed at Jamia Tayyaba Islamia Madrasa located in Gulshan Iqbal. - Establishment of Jamia Siddique Akbar: Located in Bhanguria Goth, the construction of Jamia Siddique Akbar has been finalized, featuring a fully installed marble flooring.
- Ongoing Construction in Munghopir: The Jama Masjid Imdad and Madrasa Imdad projects are currently underway in Munghopir.
- Mosques in Baluchistan: Several mosque construction projects have been completed in Baluchistan, including the Saakraan Mosque.
5. Training for Women and Youth
The Trust has organized a variety of training programs aimed at empowering women and youth to actively contribute to society.
- Programs Tailored for Women: Women have received training in social and domestic responsibilities, enabling them to nurture their families and effect positive societal changes.
- Youth Elocution Competitions: Various elocution competitions and historical awareness programs have been held to foster social consciousness among the youth.
6. Additional Welfare Activities
The Trust has organized diverse welfare activities aimed at raising social awareness and promoting social development.
- Tree Planting Campaign: A plantation campaign was initiated to emphasize the significance of environmental conservation among children and youth.
- Aid for the Underprivileged: Children were encouraged to donate old clothing and other items to assist those in need.
Conclusion
Al Imdad Welfare Foundation Trust is committed to societal improvement through educational, social, and charitable initiatives. A range of ongoing projects encompasses education, water supply, employment, healthcare facilities, and marriage assistance. The Trust remains dedicated to advancing social welfare and development with the support of the community.
